Select each of the headings to learn more about the causes of slips, trips and falls in the workplace.
Slippery surfaces

Wet or oily floor surfaces can cause injuries from slips.

Damaged flooring

Loose or uplifting mats, rugs, carpet or tiles are tripping hazards.

Poor visibility

An obstruction of view (due to objects or rubbish) increases the chance of collisions.

Bad lighting

Poor lighting can hide hazards and make it difficult to see where you're going.

Cords, leads and ropes

Loose cables, leads, or ropes can be a tripping hazard if not properly secured.

Things left open

Desk drawers and cabinets left open or unevenly weighted can cause injuries.

Rough surfaces

Uneven or damaged floor surfaces can be a tripping hazard.
