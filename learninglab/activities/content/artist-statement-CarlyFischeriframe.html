From Little Things Big Things Grow

From Little Things Big Things Grow is a sculptural that reflects on how contemporary drives towards a more cultural, environmental and political awareness in Australia have been used by corporations to market their products. Drawing on the famous protest song by Paul Kelly and Kev Carmody, the installation asks whether such cultural resistance can escape being , commodified and sold back to us as gentrified lifestyle packages. In the installation, these products and the objects they are reassembled, remixed and reconstructed as strange hybrid sculptural . Shape-shifting between home furnishings, totems, makeshift armatures and props, the sculptures on the complex and often contradictory repurposing of cultural resistance in a contemporary Australian .
