Copying another student’s answers in an assessment, or allowing another student to copy answers from you
This includes making up data or inaccurately reporting interviews or surveys.
Taking unauthorised materials into an examination
This includes buying a new or pre-written paper online, or accepting a new or pre-written paper from someone else, with or without paying for this. This includes former students and individuals from outside the university.
Sitting an examination for another student or having another person sit an examination for you
This includes accessing and sharing past exam papers that haven’t been made available to all students.
Unauthorised material may include non-approved calculators and notes, Wi-Fi devices with stored information or communication devices. This does not include materials that you need to complete the exam, such as authorised calculators or medical equipment
This includes sharing answers for an in-class assessment.
This applies to exams held in a physical exam room and to online exams.

Obtaining or purchasing work from other people or internet services and presenting it as your own for the purpose of assessment or credit
Dishonestly obtaining, using and sharing information about an assessment or examination
