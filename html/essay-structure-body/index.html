Teams have become integral to the way in which organisations function.Currently, much of the work of industry and other organisations reflects the output of teams (Maxwell 2011). According to Lau (2009), these teams underpin the structure of many organisations. Today teams have become central to many organisations, contributing in many ways such as setting goals, budgeting, solving operational problems and building effective interpersonal relations. There are significant benefits of teams for organisations, including reduced costs, creativity, new ideas and new policies (Naumes 1992).
