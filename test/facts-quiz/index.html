Casting the line

Answer the following questions regarding fact-checking to see how many fish you can catch.

    Is the following a fact, opinion or a combination?

Well done! You’ve caught a lot of fish today!

In summary, to stay alert to bias, consider the following questions when assessing any piece of information:

  • Are the purported facts reliable?
  • Are there multiple sources quoted?
  • Do the sources quoted offer differing points of view?
  • Has a counter argument or opposing view been presented?
  • Is the information presented with colourful or exaggerated language?
  • After reading an article, read the headline again. Does it fairly represent the content of the article?
  • Check yourself: are you expressing bias by agreeing with content simply because it reflects your own world view?
